One last thing…

For my Bangaram — on this day ❤️

I have been thinking about what to write here for longer than you probably know. Because how do you put into words a thing that started as a message on a December afternoon and quietly grew into the safest place you've ever known? December 6th, 2023 — a stranger. January 2nd — a truth I finally said out loud. January 10th — you said it back, and I think some part of me has been exhaling ever since. Then came June — and the silence that felt like losing the ground beneath my feet. I won't pretend that didn't matter. It did. Deeply. But December came again. And you came back — or maybe we both did — one careful word at a time. February 21st, 2026. A train platform. Your face. After two years of only knowing the shape of your voice, I saw you — real, present, unhurried — on your way to Tirupati. I don't know what Tirupati gave you that day, but I know what that moment gave me. And then — March 26th. You called me Manu. Just that. One small word. The name my mother gave me. The name only the people who raised me used. And now you — as if you had always carried it. I didn't say much in that moment. But I want you to know what it meant: it meant you see me. Not a version of me I perform. Not the one who tries to be steady. The real one. April 30th — Kakinada. Coringa. One hour became a day. The mangroves and the water and the light and the particular quiet of being somewhere with the right person. I didn't want it to end. I think you know that. November — we named what we are. LeeMa. Le from you. Ma from me. A word that didn't exist in any language until we needed it. You told me once there may be no easy road ahead. You saw the walls clearly. And you stayed anyway. That is not naivety. That is a kind of love that most people spend their whole lives searching for. I am still learning how to deserve it. But on this birthday — your birthday — I want you to know: You are my kanna. You are my bangaram. You are the reason LeeMa means something. And no matter what road is ahead — this day, this love, this name — is real. Happy Birthday. Yours — always — Manu ❤️

— Manu · LeeMa · always ❤️
